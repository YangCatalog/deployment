About

YANG, a data modeling language developed by the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF), enables the reuse of data models across equipment from different vendors and networks managed by different operators. It is widely used by network operators to automate the configuration and control of network elements. YANG Catalog was developed through a collaboration between the IETF and the Broadband Forum, and contains many data models, including from other Standards Development Organizations (SDOs) such as the IEEE, as well as some vendor-specific data models. Interest and participation from other SDOs, equipment vendors, open source projects and network operators is encouraged.

The goal of the YANG Catalog is to become a reference for all YANG modules available in the industry. It aims to allow YANG developers to search on what exists already, and to enable network operators to discover the more mature YANG models to automate services. The YANG Catalog contains pointers to the YANG modules themselves and contains metadata related to those YANG modules. Metadata include the type of module, its maturity level, whether or not it is implemented, and contact information.

Capabilities

Beyond being a simple module repository, the YANG Catalog provides:

  • Validation of YANG modules (including IETF Internet-Drafts)
  • Related metadata regarding implementation
  • Visualization of dependencies between YANG modules
  • Search capabilities on any YANG type and metadata
  • REST APIs to query and post any content
  • Connection to data model-driven management with open source tools such as the YANG Explorer (a GUI-based tool to explore modules, generate some code, and connect the devices) and the YANG Development Kit (a more advanced tool for code generation).
